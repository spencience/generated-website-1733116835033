LinkedIn: The Ontology of Professional Networks

LinkedIn represents a fascinating case study in how digital platforms shape professional identity and career trajectories. Our research examines the ontological implications of reducing human professional experience to standardized profiles and connections.

Key Research Findings

  • The emergence of "digital professional self" as distinct from physical workplace identity
  • Impact of algorithmic connection suggestions on career path development
  • Role of endorsements and skills validation in professional ontology
  • Network effects on career opportunity distribution

Critical Analysis

Our research reveals tensions between authentic professional relationships and platformized networking. The standardization of professional identity through LinkedIn's interface creates both opportunities and constraints for individual career development.
